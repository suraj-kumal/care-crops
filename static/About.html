About Agriculture

Agriculture is the backbone of Nepal's economy, providing employment to around 60% of the population and contributing approximately 25% to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP). The countryâ€™s major crops include rice, maize, wheat, millet, and barley, with rice being the staple food. Additionally, Nepal cultivates cash crops such as tea, coffee, sugarcane, jute, and cardamom, which contribute to the economy. Most farmers rely on traditional farming methods, practicing subsistence agriculture due to limited access to modern technology. Nepal's diverse agro-climatic zones allow for the cultivation of a wide variety of crops and support livestock farming, including cattle, buffalo, goats, and poultry. However, the agricultural sector faces challenges like small landholdings, lack of irrigation, low productivity, and vulnerability to climate change. To address these issues, the government and various organizations are promoting modern farming techniques, organic agriculture, and agri-business opportunities. Efforts are also being made to improve food security and boost agricultural exports. With proper investments and policies, Nepal has great potential to develop a more sustainable and productive agricultural sector. Enhancing rural infrastructure and access to financial support can further strengthen the livelihoods of Nepalese farmers.

Featured Crops

Rice

Rice is the most important staple crop in Nepal, primarily grown in the Terai and hilly regions. It provides food security and income for millions of farmers. However, rice production faces major challenges due to leaf diseases such as bacterial leaf blight and rice blast. These diseases cause leaf discoloration, weakening the plant and reducing grain yield. Farmers struggle with lower productivity and increased costs for disease management, which affects their livelihood. Climate change and poor irrigation facilities further aggravate the problem, making rice farming more vulnerable.

Tomato

Tomatoes are widely cultivated in Nepal for both household consumption and commercial purposes. They are an important vegetable crop but are highly susceptible to leaf diseases such as early blight, late blight, and tomato leaf curl virus. These diseases cause leaf wilting, yellowing, and premature fruit drop, leading to significant yield losses. Farmers often suffer from financial setbacks as they need to spend more on pesticides and disease control measures. The lack of awareness and access to resistant seed varieties worsens the situation, affecting the income of small-scale farmers.

Corn

Corn is another essential crop grown in Nepal, especially in the mid-hills and Terai regions. It is a staple food and a source of animal feed, supporting both food security and the livestock industry. However, diseases like northern corn leaf blight and maize rust severely impact corn production. These diseases lead to leaf lesions, stunted growth, and poor grain quality, reducing overall yield. Farmers face economic difficulties due to crop loss and increased production costs for disease control. Without proper disease management and improved agricultural practices, corn farming remains at risk, affecting the livelihoods of many Nepalese farmers.
